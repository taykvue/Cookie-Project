Cooking Procedure

The following procedure was used to make the coconut turtle matcha cookie.

  1. Combine wet ingredients (brown sugar, egg, honey, vanilla extract, butter, ginger crystals) in a bowl under boiling water (be cautious of hot bowl) until it reaches temperature of 160 Fahrenheit, feel the texture to see if the sugar has dissolved using your fingers, if it’s grainy, then it’s not ready.
  2. When the wet ingredient mixture is cooled down, pour mixture into a food processor and process the wet ingredients for 2 minutes or until the mixture is smooth and homogeneous.
  3. Pour all the dry ingredients (flour, baking soda, salt, matcha powder) into another bowl and carefully mix the ingredients together with a spatula.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients onto dry ingredients, and mix thoroughly, then place a plastic wrap on top of it and leave the dough in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  5. Make the filling base by adding all of the base ingredients, excluding the Medjool dates, into a bowl and stir the mixture using same method as described above, then when mixture has cooled down, pour the mixture into the food processor, add the Medjool dates (that has been pitted, seeds removed, and warmed, using lukewarm water), and process it for 3–5 minutes, or until mixture is smooth and homogeneous.
  6. Make the cocoa filling by adding the cocoa powder to half of the batter filling and purple sweet potato filling by adding the purple sweet potato powder to the other half of the batter fillings.
  7. Form the dough into a ball the core fillings (cocoa) and sweet potato filling, (covering core cocoa powder filling) then form the legs of the turtle and head, and place the ball on top of the bottom body of the legs and head structure.
  8. Before baking, put the batch in the freezer for 15 minutes while preheating the oven at 350 Fahrenheit.
  9. Bake for 10–12 minutes or until the cookie is cooked.
  10. After cooking, smear a tablespoon of sweet potato filling on top of the shell of turtle, and sprinkle coconut flakes.
